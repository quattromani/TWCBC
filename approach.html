Our Approach to Developing this Style Guide

This style guide was developed out of a need for consistency across communications emanating from Time Warner Cable Business Class in all the important places: in de facto business oriented pages on the main site, imagery and messaging connected to campaigns and their corresponding mini-sites, as well as email marketing and social media campaigns

Research

The TWC Busines Class Marketing performed market research of organizational styleguides found all around the Internet and created an outline to start from.

Form and function were of specific concern to ensure the site was easy to navigate to find vital information. Insight from an independent contractor especially familiar with the development of online style guides was also sought out.

Development

The style guide has been developed to promote the design and tone ideals that it advocates.

From a functional perspective, it was developed by an external contractor and is now posted to the TWC web environment using injection components that allow it to mimic the intended look and feel.

Evaluation

This style guide is a work in progress albeit it is meant to stand as a benchmark for the issues it addresses.

The TWCBC Marketing team stands ready to discuss the items found on this site, and maintains an open mind when it comes to these kinds of discussions: what works now may change based on new technology, overall company positioning, or simply common sense.

The Brand Experience

The truth is that "brand" is more than a company's logo or colors: it's a feeling derived from the combination of imagery and graphical elements, and the tone and messages of the written content.

Establishing this brand experience and maintaining consistency is important, which is what this style guide is designed to help with.
