Building an effective online presence for Time Warner Cable Business Class takes many skills. The Digital Web Marketing Team provides web standards and editorial guidelines to help provide guidance for the architecture, design, and content of digital assets that are part of business.twc.com.

This information is provided to content creators, agencies, and internal stakeholders to assist in developing consistent and excellent digital content experiences for visitors to all TWCBC websites, landing pages, and social media environments.

The Digital Web Marketing Team produced and maintains this Guide. We recognize that this is a work in progress, and we will keep an open mind about the introduction of new content/design parameters. What works now will certainly change over time as a result of new technology, company positioning, digital experience best practices, and common sense.

Principles

Content and design on the website should adhere to these principles:

Defer to Content

Give the content center stage in the layout. Design for experiences that are unobtrusive and authentically thoughtful, in order to respect what a visitor needs

Make the Useful Beautiful

Utility satisfies needs, but beauty drives desire. Design for experiences that people love to have. Create aesthetically pleasing offerings that carry a consistent meaning in their form and function.

Design for Flexibility

Patterns and designs are made to be easily adaptable. These guidelines encourage consistency over uniformity -- providing a sense of familiarity and ease with the brand, while still allowing for customization of each content asset.

Inform and Delight

Our visitors are smart. Engage them in moments of inspiration and curiosity. Design for enlightening experiences that encourage critical thinking.

Be Contextually Aware

Be mindful of what users need by providing them the right tools at the right time. Design content for the most desirable outcomes while keeping changing context in mind.

Brand Promise

“Time Warner Cable Business Class enables companies to launch their business’s possibilities.”

This is our Brand Promise. We achieve this through our highly skilled people and reliable technology that is designed to help our customers operate more effectively, efficiently and ultimately be more successful in their business.

Yes, we are a technology company, but there is a human being behind everything we do. Behind every truck roll, every advertisement, every phone call and website interaction, there is a human. We aren’t businesses talking to businesses; we are humans seeking to connect with humans.

Brand Experience

Much more than a company's logo or color palette, Brand is the collection of experiences in the heart and mind of the customer. Each encounter a visitor has with our company is a brand experience. Our brand reflects who we are today, and is aspirational of where we will lead our customers and prospects.

Strong and consistent brand identification improves market position, drives customer satisfaction, and increases profitability. Our website is the most powerful, flexible, and accessible element of our brand collateral, and our most effective tool for lead generation

To provide authentically thoughtful experiences in our content and on our website, we must demonstrate that we are not product-centered, or even user-centered: we must be human-centered. We must know people first as people, not as prospects or customers. We must understand what drives them and is of value to them in order to bring our brand promise to life

Segment Audiences

Small Businesses

Less than 25 employees

Primary Decision Makers:
  • Owners
  • Office Managers

Key Motivations

  • Achieve business and personal goals (e.g. success, freedom)
  • Leverage technology to stay connected both inside and outside the office
  • Keep their business up and running
  • Meet changing customer demands and expectations.

Mid-Market Businesses

25-499 employees

Primary Decision Makers:
  • IT Managers
  • IT Consultants
  • Owners

Key Motivations

  • Position their business for optimal success today and tomorrow
  • Solve challenges hurting the business and customers
  • Answer needs for improvement while minimizing risk
  • Create a solid business case addressing financial realities

Enterprise Businesses

500+ employees

Primary Decision Makers:
  • IT Managers/Directors
  • CIO, CTO
  • IT Consultants

Key Motivations

  • Leverage technology to move their business forward
  • Understand and be able to balance promise, risk, return
  • Influence stakeholders to make the necessary technology changes
  • Create the business case for desired/needed solutions.
