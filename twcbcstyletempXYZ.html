            Welcome to the Time Warner Cable Business Class Style Guide

            This style guide is designed to help content creators working on behalf of Time Warner Cable Business Class (TWCBC)—be they internal partners and employees or external agencies—make decisions around look, feel, tone, and style of communications that are labeled as coming from TWCBC.

            The goal of this site is to provide guidance and resources to unify all messaging and imagery under the banner of Time Warner Cable Business Class, ensuring that our customers’ experience is consistent time after time.

            What is a "Style Guide?"

            A style guide's primary purpose is the serve as a rubric for documents distributed by an organization. Its collection of guidelines and parameters keep all communication consistent in both appearance and writing style. Style guides are absolutely essential to large organizations such as Time Warner Cable and Time Warner Cable Business Class, as our employees are spread across the United States and all have varying degrees of experience working with the TWC or TWCBC brands due to skill level and/or longevity with the company.

            Done well, a style guide should not feel restrictive. It should instead give all parties working on communications for the brand a confidence in their decisions, not to mention a reference point when something "doesn't quite feel right."

            How is this Style Guide Organized?

            This site it organized into two sections: Editorial Framework and Toolkit.

            Editorial Framework

            This portion of the style guide covers the “why” of creating marketing: it is designed to help content creators craft the personality of marketing and communications from TWCBC. It includes things like brand colors, key messages, typography, image selection guidelines, and voice and tone commentary.

            Toolkit

            The toolkit section of the style guide covers the “how” of creating marketing materials, especially for the Web. In this section you will find specifics on developing web page components and ensuring good alignment with the style as defined by this style guide.

            Questions? Concerns?

            This style guide should serve as the arbiter in style disputes: attempts have been made to vet recommendations included here among key stakeholders within TWCBC and guidance here should be considered a “final ruling.”
