The TWCBC Digital Style Guide is a set of living guidelines that communicate our brand promise in all digital communications with consistency of style and unified messaging.

These guidelines are designed to ensure that our website and digital visitor experiences are on message every time, and to help content creators make consistent and brand-appropriate decisions around tone, voice, messaging, image selection, and creative execution.

Why a Digital Style Guide?

Brand, creative and content guidelines are absolutely essential to large organizations such ours. Consistency and brand messaging can suffer if guidelines aren’t in place. Especially in the digital space, content development is a team-wide effort and must be supported by the right tools and understanding. Our digital audience has unique expectations that shape every piece of content we create. Most importantly, a Digital Style Guide will:

  1. Put our Audiences First.

    Staying in touch with visitor behaviors and activities dictates the language, mediums, and messages we use. Guidelines recognize a link between what our audience needs and the best way to fulfill that need in the most effective way.

  2. Breed Consistency.

    Giving our audience a consistent experience builds loyalty and trust. Guidelines can be a central reference to keep the overall voice/tone consistent, and to ensure creative output reflects the quality of the written content.

  3. Create Better Content.

    A Digital Style Guide is both a practical resource as well as a commitment to well-considered content. Personality, trends, brand values, key messaging, tone, style, image selection and formatting all contribute to the quality of our content – and guidelines keep this all in check.

Organization

This Style Guide is organized into four sections:

Approach

Understanding our audience, brand platform, and message hierarchy enables consistent content and creative. Our design and content principles are outlined here, which reinforce our brand and provide a seamless digital communication experience.

Editorial Guide

The Editorial Guide provides resources to guide the voice, tone, grammar, mechanics, design, images, and visual assets for your content.

Here you’ll also find important tips for working with the Digital Web Marketing Team to publish content to the website.

Toolkit

The Toolkit section covers technical details, components, and specifics of the Adobe CMS for page authors and developers.

Also included here are development best practices for working with our delivery partners.

Working with Digital Web Marketing

The TWCBC Digital Web Marketing Team manages updates and advises on website content, product and campaign placement, and provides creative and editorial guidance. Our processes and standards are easy to follow. Also, we are happy to share the candy we keep in the Hive.
